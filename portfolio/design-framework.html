Content Design & Development Framework

Project Management / 2014 - 2017

How might we produce a consistent level of quality and more predictable timelines for our users and clients?

Objective

Design a framework for rapidly designing and building dozens of consistently high-quality microlearning experiences.

Tabletop simulation playtest session.
Waterfall user research and design process.

My Role

UX Designer & Project Manager

My contributions to the team's efforts primarily came in leading design thinking activities, documenting our insights, and defining our program-wide instructional design system.

Design Tools Used:
Customer Journey Mapping, Card Sorting, Dale's Cone of Experience, Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

The Story

We started the project with two primary resources: (1) an existing, three-day in-person tabletop leadership simulation and (2) a team of experienced leadership coaches that have facilitated the simulation for several years. We leveraged these resources through playtesting, detailed task analysis, and user observation. As a result, the team grew to understand and appreciate every facet of the tabletop simulation that contributed to a successful user experience.

Detailed interaction flow chart.

Our primary finding was that only about 23% of users reported a state of 'flow', where the balance of challenge and success created a sense of immersion and growth. Through discussion with stakeholders, we determined that number to be too low, and established the definition of success for our Digital Leadership Challenge Simulation to be closer to 40% of users reporting a state of flow.

Key Design Insights

Discussion is King. Every interaction in the simulation should contribute to group discussion.

The experience should be dynamic and unpredictable, just like change is in the real world.

The simulation should be designed to be modular, so that the experience can be customized per client.

Design Artifacts

Result

After eight months of research, design iterations, game development, and user testing, a fully-functioning Minimally Viable Product was completed and delivered to live clients during three daylong pilot sessions. Early feedback indicated a marked improvement in 'flow'-state for users.
